Curriculum Vitae


Christophe Servan is AI Principal Scientist at Intescia since 2024 and part-time lecturer at Polytech'Saclay since 2024. He is part of the head of ATALA, the French National association for NLP since 2020.

His main interest is multilingual Natural Language Processing jointly with Deep Learning for Information Retrieval. He focuses on model context for several fields of NLP: semantic content analysis and model, machine translation, Intent Detection in real-life application at scale.

From 2022 to 2024, he was part of the LISN-CNRS lab part of the LIPS team

Before joining Intescia, he was Research Manager and Scientist at Qwant from 2018 to 2024. In the meanwhile, he was deputy director of the INRIA-Qwant Lab from 2019 to 2021. He was formely applied researcher at SYSTRAN to work on domain adaptation for NMT (2016-2018), member of the GETALP team at the University of Grenoble to develop Deep Learning approaches for SMT evaluation (2015-2016), part of the Xerox Research Centre Europe (XRCE) in the Machine Learning for Document Access and Translation (MLDAT) group (2013-2015), postdoc researcher in the Speech and Language Technology group at the laboratory of the University of Le Mans, France (LIUM), research engineer at the Vision & Content Engineering Laboratory (LVIC) at the CEA-LIST (2009-2010).

He received a PhD in Computer Science from the University of Avignon, France in 2008.
